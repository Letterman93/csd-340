Chapter 1: A Little About Me

I was born in Wake Forest, North Carolina, but moved to Florida when I was six after my dad took a new job and the company relocated our family. I grew up in West Palm Beach and spent all of my school years there before graduating high school in 2012. My original plan was to attend a Florida university for architectural engineering, but the cost changed that path pretty quickly.

After high school, my family moved back to North Carolina, and I decided to join the Air Force while taking a couple of semesters at Southwestern Community College. Once my job became available, I went through basic training in Texas and was eventually stationed at Lackland AFB, Keesler AFB, and Peterson AFB in Colorado. While in service, I worked as an Emergency Actions Specialist in a role focused on critical operations, which gave me a unique look into high-level decision making and real-time global events.

I met my wife in Colorado in 2016, and we had our daughter while I was still in the Air Force. After completing six years of service, I separated in 2019, and shortly after we welcomed our son. I transitioned into civilian roles including project management and data work, eventually becoming a data engineer working on AI and machine learning projects tied to government systems.

In 2020, we moved to Nebraska and built a home, but over time we realized we wanted a different lifestyle. We sold the house, bought a fifth wheel, and now travel full-time while raising our two kids and focusing on school and family. Long term, my goal is to build a software business that I can run while traveling, and eventually settle down on land to build a homestead.
